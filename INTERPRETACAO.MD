### 3. The Entropic Force

In thermodynamics, an entropic force $F$ arises in a macroscopic system that tends to increase its entropy $S$. For a virtual displacement $\Delta x$ and temperature $T$:

$$ F \Delta x = T \Delta S $$

#### 3.1. Unruh Temperature
An observer in an accelerated frame $a$ perceives the vacuum as a thermal bath with temperature:

$$ T = \frac{\hbar a}{2 \pi c k_B} $$

#### 3.2. Derivation of Newton's Law
Considering the change in holographic entropy caused by a mass $M$ moving near the screen:

1.  The associated energy is equipartitioned across the $N$ bits: $E = \frac{1}{2} N k_B T = M c^2$.
2.  Substituting $T$ and $N$:
    $$ M c^2 = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{A c^3}{4 G \hbar} \right) \left( \frac{\hbar a}{2 \pi c k_B} \right) k_B $$
3.  Simplifying and knowing that $A = 4 \pi r^2$:
    $$ M = \frac{a r^2}{G} \implies a = \frac{G M}{r^2} $$

We recover $F=ma$ and the Universal Law of Gravitation **without postulating gravity**. It is an inevitable consequence of information's tendency to maximize entropy.

$$ g \approx \sqrt{g_N a_0} = \frac{\sqrt{G M a_0}}{r} $$
